Journaled

Track your journal subscriptions in one place and download the latest content.

While searching through library databases and cross-database indexes is a great way to do research on a topic, when it comes to staying up to date on all the latest research from your discipline's popular journals, there's no easy way to do it. Rather than try and subscribe to “new issue” alerts, Journaled monitors CrossRef for new DOIs from your subscribed journals. From the app, you can see all the latest issues (refreshed every 6 hours) and get notified by email if there's a new issue.

To go the extra mile, you can download the PDF from SciHub and add the citation to your Reference Manager (Zotero) directly from the web application. The app is open source and can be created in one click from the heroku button.

Noted

Automatically store and classify your field notes with machine learning

As I describe in my writing, audio recordings from your smartphone are one of the most useful tools. Many apps will transcribe the audio for you and let you share that text transcription. Noted allows you to email any text to the app, and it will store an classify your field notes, picking out Names, Places, Topics, People and other important entities so that you can easily find the topics that are relevant to your research reports, books, and articles.

The app is hosted on the web, and you can create your own version of the app, or feel free to use the app yourself.
